Post 1: Forms

For class we wee tasked with reading this article on form design by Salim Ansari. The article was insightful and will be a useful reference for UX work. When designing a form it is crucial to keep all questions straight to the point and easy to understand. A good design will have minimal input fields. A single column layout will present the information without overloading ther user. Studies have also shown that breaking down a form into steps will also prevent visual noise. The simplest questions should be asked at the beginning of the form and slowly ease the user into the more complicated ones. It is also noted to explain specific input requirements such as, case sensitivity or character limitations for a username. All labels should be inline and above input fields for effective visual clarity.The proximity of each label should leave ample amounts of space between it and the input field. Input fields should also be sized to the amount of information asked for in order to not confuse the user. Drop down menus should be used very sparingly if at all.

Post 5: Game Development

For class this week we're tasked with reading this article on mobile game development by Amir Dori. Dori map's out various UX patterns and techniques to design a mobile games. The designer has gained years of expierence working for different companies and breaks downs his methodology into ten different categories; UI Positions, Sliders, Pop-ups, User Choices, Rewarded Videos, Draggable Objects, Intro Videos, Rate-Us Diaglog, and Store Dialog. He discusses how essential studying user-data and feedback is essential for impriving the product. The article also briefly touches upon the idea- of dark-UX, creating in-game assets that can lead to further monetization such as, In-Game Store, More Apps, and Ads. These buttons are included into the design to even be clicked on by accident. I find this type of practice to be questionable and will harm the long term usability of the product for the user.The overarching goal is to create an easy to understand and enjoyable expierence for players. Doing so will maximize revenue and keep players engaged in the product. The higher qaulity of a design made with the user in mind is what I believe to be the most effective mindset to have in mind. This article is an excellent reference I'll be reading through again due to my own interests in mobile game design and development.
